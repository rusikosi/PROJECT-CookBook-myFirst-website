Welcome

This is my friend Ivan, and this site is my Friend's Cookbook.

His family didn't live rich, they were very poor. When he was a teenager, he really needed some pocket money, but his parents didn't have a single extra cent. Then he found himself a summer job, he became an assistant cook in a restaurant.

He was very lucky with the chef - she was a very strict lady, but she knew everything about cooking. My friend respected and admired her, and he liked working in the kitchen more and more.

So he decided to get a degree and become a professional chef, forever linking his life with cooking.



I've chosen three great desserts that Ivan made at different times
and posted them on this site for you to make them too and enjoy the beauty and taste!

Lime Pie

Time Portions
1 hour 6 pcs.

Ingredients

  • 420 g condensed milk
  • 150 ml of freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 200 g sugar
  • 60 ml water
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 1 lime (zest)
  • 3 egg whites
  • 60 ml water
  • vanilla sugar
  • salt
  • shortcrust pastry of your choice

    • Cooking

  • In a large bowl, mix condensed milk with sugar and egg yolks. Add the lime juice and whisk until smooth. Stir in the lime zest.
  • Pour the filling into the pre-baked crust. Bake until the outer edges of the pie are firm but the center of the pie looks slightly runny, 15 to 20 minutes.
  • In a large bowl, use a hand mixer to beat the egg whites on high speed until they are white and frothy and have soft peaks.
  • Meanwhile, in a saucepan, mix ¼ cup (60 milliliters) of water and the sugar over high heat until the sugar dissolves and the syrup comes to a boil. The temperature on the thermometer should read 116°C, this is the soft ball stage - if you gently drop a little syrup into a glass of cold water, it will form a soft, pliable ball. Remove from the heat.
  • With the mixer running, thinly pour the hot sugar syrup into the whipped whites. Continue to whisk until the meringue holds the shape of the whisk as it rotates. The texture should be voluminous and silky. If it looks runny and sticky, continue whipping until it hardens.
  • Add the vanilla and salt and whisk until they are blended.
  • When the filling is ready, place the meringue on top of the cake. Use the back of a hot, wet spoon to create a wavy texture with peaks and troughs, or transfer the meringue to a pastry bag with a fluted nozzle and squeeze it out, starting at the outer edge and moving toward the center.
  • Turn on the oven to 200°. The heat of the oven varies greatly from oven to oven, so keep a close eye on the pie the entire time it is under the broiler, gently turning the tray as needed to evenly toast the meringue. Heat until the meringue peaks are golden brown, 1 to 3 minutes, depending on your oven.
  • Send the pie to the refrigerator until completely cooled, about 4 hours. If serving, slice hot with a wet knife.

    Guinness Cake

    Time Portions
    1,5 hour 6 pcs.

    Ingredients

    Biscuit
  • 1 t flour
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 0,5 tc cocoa
  • 1 tbsp baking soda
  • 0,5 tbsp sour cream
  • 110 g butter
  • 1 tbsp guinness
  • salt

    • Frosting

  • 110 g butter
  • 130 g cream cheese
  • 0,5 tbsp powdered sugar
  • 0,5 tbsp apple cider

    • Cooking

  • Mix flour, sugar, cocoa, baking soda and salt in a small bowl.
  • In a large bowl, beat eggs and sour cream. Add butter and beer gently.
  • Mix flour mixture with an egg scoop
  • Transfer to mold and bake for 35 minutes at 180° or until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean. Allow to cool.
  • While the biscuit is baking, prepare the frosting: Mix the soft butter with the cheese with the mixer.
  • Add the biscuit powder and cider and mix. Put in the fridge.
  • Spread the frosting evenly on the cooled biscuit

    Rhubarb Pie

    Time Portions
    1 hour 20 min 6 pcs.

    Ingredients

    Biscuit
  • 2,5 tbsp flour
  • 170g butter
  • 0,5 tbsp water
  • salt

    • Stuffing

  • 900 g rhubarb
  • 1 tbsp strawberries
  • 1 tbsp blueberries
  • 1 t sugar
  • 2,5 tbsp cornstrach
  • salt

    • Cooking

  • Combine flour, sugar and salt in a large bowl
  • Stir the soft but still cold butter into the flour.
  • Gradually add iced water a spoonful at a time, stirring into the dough
  • Cut the dough into 2 equal portions, wrap in clingfilm and place in the refrigerator for at least an hour.
  • Cut the rhubarb into 1-1.5 cm thick slices, put them in a bowl and mix with sugar and salt, and leave for 15-30 minutes. (The longer the better.)
  • Mix with strawberries, blueberries and starch.
  • Roll out half of the dough into a sheet and place it in the molds.
  • Place rhubarb and berries in mold and cover with second half of rolled out dough
  • Seal the top layer of dough with the bottom layer and trim off any excess
  • Put the cake in the freezer for 20 minutes and preheat the oven to 200°
  • Remove the cake from the frosting and brush the top with egg.
  • Place in the oven to bake for 40 minutes, then lower the oven temperature to 170° and bake for another 20-30 minutes.
  • If the pie is browned and the filling is bubbling, it's ready!
  • Refrigerate the pie for at least 2 hours or it will fall apart.
